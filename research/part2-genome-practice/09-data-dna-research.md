## Key Findings

### 1. Designing Data-Intensive Applications — Martin Kleppmann
- **Published**: March 2017 by O'Reilly Media
- **Second edition**: Co-authored with Chris Riccomini, due late 2025 (integrating new technologies, emerging trends, GDPR)
- **Author**: Martin Kleppmann — researcher in distributed systems and security at University of Cambridge
- **Focus**: Architecture of data systems and integration into data-intensive applications
- **Topics covered**:
  - Strengths and weaknesses of different tools
  - Trade-offs: consistency, scalability, fault tolerance, complexity
  - Distributed systems research modern databases are built upon
- **Core themes**: Building applications that are **reliable, scalable, and maintainable** for long run
- **Reception**: "Bridges huge gap between distributed systems theory and practical engineering" — rare resource connecting theory and practice
- **Guidance**: Helps developers make smart decisions designing/implementing data infrastructure and systems
